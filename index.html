Abstract: We introduce Adaptive Efficiency (AE), a formally grounded metric that quantifies the rate of structural self-improvement of open cognitive systems. Grounded in Prigogine's dissipative structures, Landauer's principle, and Kolmogorov complexity theory, AE is distinguished from thermodynamic efficiency (bounded by Carnot) and standard AI performance metrics (presupposing static task distributions). The Self-Evolving Machine (SEM) is its architectural instantiation. Three independent layers of evidence support the framework: a mathematical theorem with falsifiable predictions and three explicit failure regimes; a stochastic simulation with 1,000 Monte Carlo runs demonstrating statistical dominance over static and RL baselines; and a prototype self-modifying coding agent achieving 105% relative performance improvement across 50 iterations.

Six Verifiable Contributions

  • Formal definition of Adaptive Efficiency (AE) as the time-derivative of a structured Capability Function C(t), incorporating stochastic noise, resource saturation, and failure probability — explicitly distinguished from all existing metrics.
  • Physical grounding of AE in non-equilibrium thermodynamics (Prigogine), Landauer's principle, and Kolmogorov complexity theory — establishing that self-improving AI systems are open physical systems governed by the same principles as dissipative structures in nature.
  • The Adaptive Efficiency Growth Theorem: necessary and sufficient conditions for sustained positive AE, with explicit derivation of FR-1 (Noise Dominance), FR-2 (Resource Saturation), and FR-3 (Algorithmic Incompressibility) — each corresponding to a known physical phase transition.
  • Stochastic simulation with 1,000 Monte Carlo runs, Gaussian noise injection, failure probability modeling, resource saturation ceiling, and three-way statistical comparison (p < 0.001 after Bonferroni correction).
  • Minimal working prototype: self-modifying coding agent achieving 105% relative performance improvement across 50 iterations with 5 independent random seeds — empirical proof of concept.
  • Complete system architecture with pseudocode, sensitivity analysis, ablation study, and alignment framework embedding safety as thermodynamic constraints inside the evolution loop.
